Introduction

My name is Laura Rosas and I am a videogame designer and developer specialised in programming.

I studied a degree in Video Game Computing and Engineering (Informática e Ingeniería del Videojuego) at ESNE Asturias (currently ESDAC), where I learned the basics of video game design, art and programming. Basically, they teach us everything we need to be able to make video games.

At the beginning I didn't know which branch I wanted to go for, so I tried to learn a lot about all of them in order to make a decision. This led me to make many of my games on my own so I could practice in all branches. By the last year I realized that I wanted to go for the programming branch and I started to work with artists, or using the Unity Asste Store.

After finishing at ESNE Asturias I studied one more year at ESNE (currently UDIT) in Madrid, where I got a degree in Video Game Design and Development (Diseño y Desarrollo de Videojuegos) in the programming branch.

Why video games

Like almost everyone who starts studying to make videogames, it all started because of how much I love playing videogames. Video games are a fascinating way to express yourself because, unlike other media, it's interactive. The player discovers the story as they overcomes difficulties, which can make you get even more involved in the story, because it's up to you and your skills to continue or not.

Even if you really like playing video games, in the end making them is something very different. Many people who started the degree with me dropped out in the first year when they realised that they didn't like making videogames, just playing them. On the other hand, those of us who continued, discovered something new in videogames that made us passionate about them. Now I see videogames differently, sometimes while I'm playing I start thinking about how I could replicate certain mechanics, which makes it very interesting.
