Tank Game

Technical details

Developed in Unity

Single-player

Genre: Real-time action

Platform: Computer

Summary

Video game in which you take control of a tank and you will have to make your way through several obstacles and enemies to survive.

To be the best you will have to kill as many enemies as possible.

Mechanics

The player controls the tank using the "WASD" keys. With "W" they move towards the direction the tank is facing, with "S" they move in the opposite direction. "A" and "D" rotate the tank on itself, thereby changing the direction in which the tank is facing.

The game has an upper-view camera that follows the tank.

Shot types

Normal shot: this is the fastest attack that is executed by pressing the left click of the mouse. This shot works by calculating what it hits when you hit the button and outputs a shot particle and an impact particle, without instantiating any moving objects (with a Raycast).

Missile shot: the missile is fired with the right click. In this case the firing works by instancing the missile, which will move and if it collides it explodes (it can be with walls, with enemies and it will also be destroyed after travelling a certain distance).

Each shot has a separate ammunition and is shown on the screen in the upper right corner. Below the ammunition, the remaining health of the player's tank is displayed. The player flashes red when taking damage.

Score

The game has a scoring system that is calculated by the number of enemies the player kills. Tanks have more value than soldiers. The score is displayed in the lower left corner of the screen.

The game has a system that saves the best score you have in each level.

Dangers

There are three types of dangers:

Spikes: they disappear and appear after a certain amount of time.

Saws: they move from one fixed point to another and if the player comes into contact with them they will take damage.

Water: equivalent to falling into the void, it instantly kills the player.

Enemies

There are two types of enemies, they all have a health system, when they are damaged they flash white and when they die they explode.

Soldiers: These enemies attack with the same system as the player's normal shot, they do not have missiles. If they see the player and are not in shooting range they will approach the player until they can fire. They will then stop and look at the player, shooting until the player moves away, then they will approach the player again. If the player runs over them or shoots them until they run out of life they will die.

Tanks: They work in a very similar way to soldiers, except that tanks only fire missiles, and are also unaffected if the player runs into them. They have more life than soldiers.

Items

There are three types of items:

Normal shot ammunition: increases the normal shot ammunition.

Missile shot ammunition: increases the number of missiles the player has.

Health: gives health points to the player.
